Enter the Dutch market with a local head start

Van Sas Sales & Marketing · Market Entry Netherlands

The Netherlands is one of Europe’s most attractive B2B tech markets: digitally mature, internationally minded and a natural gateway to the EU. But Dutch buyers have their own habits, their own media and their own networks. I help foreign IT, SaaS and cybersecurity companies build a go-to-market that works here — with local knowledge and a network that opens doors.

“You don’t need a bigger budget to win in the Netherlands. You need to be locally relevant.”

What makes the Dutch market different

Dutch business culture is direct, pragmatic and flat: decision makers expect substance over polish, and they do their own research long before they talk to sales. The local IT ecosystem is tight-knit, with its own trade media, analysts and communities where reputations are made. English is widely accepted in tech — but being visibly present in the Dutch conversation is what builds trust.

What I do for market entrants

I translate your global proposition into a Dutch go-to-market: positioning and messaging that resonate locally, pricing and channel choices that fit the market, and a realistic entry plan. Through my decades-long career in Dutch IT, media and cybersecurity I bring an extensive network of decision makers, journalists and analysts — which means introductions, PR opportunities in the Dutch IT trade press, and first conversations that would otherwise take months. Where useful, I act as your interim or fractional commercial leader in the Netherlands until your own team is in place.

A realistic route to traction

Successful entrants start focused: a sharply defined segment, a proposition validated with first reference customers, and only then acceleration. I help you sequence that journey — and I am honest about timelines: first meetings can happen within weeks through the right introductions, while building a structural pipeline takes months of consistent presence.

Frequently asked questions

Do we need Dutch-language materials?

In B2B tech, English materials are usually fine to start with; Dutch translations help build trust as you scale. More important than language is local relevance: understanding the market context, the competitive landscape and the buying habits of Dutch decision makers.

Can you work alongside our international team?

Yes — that is the usual setup. Your team keeps global ownership of product and brand; I add the local strategy, the network and, where needed, hands-on commercial execution in the Netherlands.
